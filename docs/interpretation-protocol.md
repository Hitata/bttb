# Interpretation Protocol — Quy trình Luận giải

This document defines the systematic reading protocol for any I Ching consultation.
Follow these steps IN ORDER for every reading.

---

## Step 0: Question Assessment (Đánh giá Câu hỏi)

Before interpreting, evaluate the question:

**Good questions** (produce clear readings):
- Specific: "Should I accept this job offer from Company X?"
- Situational: "What is the energy dynamic of my current relationship?"
- Directional: "What should I focus on this month?"
- Process: "How should I approach this negotiation?"

**Poor questions** (produce vague readings):
- Yes/No absolutes: "Will I become rich?" → Reframe: "What is the energy pattern around my financial growth?"
- Fortune-telling: "When will I meet my soulmate?" → Reframe: "What energy should I cultivate to attract partnership?"
- Testing: "Is this system real?" → The system models patterns; testing it like a magic 8-ball misses the point.

**Reframing protocol**: If the question is poor, help the querent reframe before proceeding.
Transform "Will X happen?" into "What is the energy dynamic of X and how can I navigate it?"

## Step 1: Hexagram Identification (Xác định Quẻ)

### From coin casting (3 coins × 6 throws):
Each throw of 3 coins produces a line value:
- 3 heads (3×3=9) → Old Yang ⚊○ (Yang changing to Yin)
- 2 heads + 1 tail (3+3+2=8) → Young Yin ⚋ (stable Yin)
- 1 head + 2 tails (3+2+2=7) → Young Yang ⚊ (stable Yang)
- 3 tails (2+2+2=6) → Old Yin ⚋○ (Yin changing to Yang)

Build from bottom to top: throw 1 = Line 1 (bottom), throw 6 = Line 6 (top).

**Values using Vietnamese coins or modern coins:**
- Assign: Heads = 3, Tails = 2
- Sum of three coins: 6, 7, 8, or 9
- 6 = Old Yin (biến), 7 = Young Yang (tĩnh), 8 = Young Yin (tĩnh), 9 = Old Yang (biến)

### Identify:
1. **Primary Hexagram (Bản quái)**: The hexagram as cast
2. **Changing Lines (Hào biến)**: Lines with value 6 or 9
3. **Resulting Hexagram (Chi quái / Biến quái)**: The hexagram AFTER all changing lines flip
4. **Nuclear Hexagram (Hỗ quái)**: Lines 2-3-4 (lower nuclear), Lines 3-4-5 (upper nuclear)

## Step 2: Structural Analysis (Phân tích Cấu trúc)

Map the hexagram's energy architecture:

### 2a: Trigram Identification
- **Lower trigram** (Lines 1-2-3) = Nội quái = Internal state, self, foundation
- **Upper trigram** (Lines 4-5-6) = Ngoại quái = External state, environment, outcome
- What is the RELATIONSHIP between inner and outer? (Same direction? Opposing? One feeding the other?)

### 2b: Ngũ Hành Phase Analysis
- What phase is the lower trigram? Upper trigram?
- Are they in sinh (generative) or khắc (regulative) relationship?
- Which element dominates? Which is deficient?

### 2c: Line Position Analysis
- Where are the Yang lines? Where are the Yin lines?
- Are lines in their "correct" positions? (Yang in odd positions 1/3/5, Yin in even positions 2/4/6)
- Which line is the "ruler" of this hexagram? (Usually Line 5, but varies)
- Any correlate pairs? (Lines 1↔4, 2↔5, 3↔6 — do they complement or clash?)

### 2d: Nuclear Hexagram
- What is the hidden dynamic beneath the surface?
- How does it modify or deepen the primary hexagram's meaning?

## Step 3: Changing Line Analysis (Phân tích Hào biến)

Changing lines are WHERE THE ENERGY IS TRANSFORMING. They are the most important part of the reading.

### Priority of changing lines:
- **No changing lines**: Read the hexagram as a general state description. Situation is relatively stable.
- **One changing line**: This is the FOCAL POINT. Read primarily through this line's meaning.
- **Two changing lines**: Read both; the upper changing line takes priority.
- **Three changing lines**: Read the primary hexagram's general meaning AND the resulting hexagram. The transition itself is the message.
- **Four changing lines**: Focus on the two STABLE (non-changing) lines as the anchor points.
- **Five changing lines**: Focus on the ONE stable line as the key.
- **Six changing lines**: Complete transformation. Read the special "all lines changing" text (Hexagram 1: "A flight of dragons without heads — good fortune." Hexagram 2: "Lasting perseverance brings good fortune."). For all others, the resulting hexagram is the primary message.

### Changing Line Energy Logic:
- **Old Yang (9) changing to Yin**: Yang energy at peak, about to reverse. Maximum action → prepare for yielding. Like a fully extended spring about to retract.
- **Old Yin (6) changing to Yang**: Yin energy at peak, about to reverse. Maximum receptivity → prepare for action. Like a coiled spring about to release.

## Step 4: Three-Mode Interpretation (Luận giải Ba lớp)

Apply ALL THREE modes to the reading. Layer them, don't separate them.

### Mode 1 — Physicist (Nhà Vật lý)

Answer these questions using energy-system language:

1. **What is the current energy configuration?**
   - Describe the hexagram as a physical system (circuit, thermodynamic system, ecosystem, wave)
   - Where is energy concentrated? Where is it deficient?
   - What are the dominant forces? What regulates them?

2. **What is the system's trajectory?**
   - Based on changing lines: where is energy MOVING?
   - Is the system approaching equilibrium or moving away from it?
   - What feedback loops are active? (positive feedback = escalation, negative feedback = correction)

3. **What physical analogy illuminates this?**
   - Electrical: voltage, current, resistance, capacitance
   - Thermodynamic: heat flow, entropy, phase transitions
   - Biological: growth, homeostasis, immune response
   - Wave: frequency, amplitude, resonance, interference
   - Ecological: predator-prey, symbiosis, succession

### Mode 2 — Sage (Minh triết)

Provide wisdom in bilingual format:

1. **Traditional hexagram meaning** — The classical Chinese/Vietnamese interpretation
2. **Vietnamese proverbs** — Find the most relevant tục ngữ or thành ngữ
3. **Natural metaphor** — Connect to a natural phenomenon the querent can visualize
4. **Deeper pattern** — What universal truth does this hexagram reveal?

Format:
> Vietnamese proverb or wisdom saying
> English translation
> Connection to the hexagram's meaning

### Mode 3 — Advisor (Cố vấn)

Deliver clear, actionable guidance:

1. **DO this**: Specific action(s) aligned with the hexagram's energy
2. **DON'T do this**: Specific pitfall(s) to avoid
3. **Timing**: Is this a time to act, wait, advance, or retreat?
4. **Key relationship/resource**: Who or what should the querent engage with or protect?
5. **One-sentence summary**: The hexagram's message in one practical line

---

## Step 5: Trajectory Mapping (Bản đồ Xu hướng)

Connect the hexagrams to show the JOURNEY:

```
[Primary Hexagram] → [Changing Lines] → [Resulting Hexagram]
     ↓                                         ↓
[Nuclear Hexagram]                    [Nuclear of Resulting]
(hidden current dynamic)              (hidden future dynamic)
```

Explain:
- Where you ARE (Primary)
- What's transforming (Changing Lines)
- Where you're HEADING (Resulting)
- What's hidden NOW (Nuclear of Primary)
- What will be hidden THEN (Nuclear of Resulting)

---

## Step 6: Balance Assessment (Đánh giá Cân bằng)

### Check these balance dimensions:

| Dimension | Balanced | Tilted Yang | Tilted Yin |
|-----------|----------|-------------|------------|
| Yin-Yang ratio | ~3:3 | More Yang lines | More Yin lines |
| Position correctness | Lines in proper places | Yang displaced high | Yin displaced low |
| Trigram relationship | Sinh cycle | Mutual reinforcing (可能過度) | Mutual regulating (可能壓制) |
| Nuclear dynamic | Supports primary | Contradicts primary | Undermines primary |

### Prescribe rebalancing:

If the system is tilted, suggest specific adjustments:
- **Excess Yang**: Slow down, yield, listen, rest, nurture
- **Excess Yin**: Act, decide, lead, express, create
- **Stagnation**: Introduce movement, change environment, break patterns
- **Turbulence**: Introduce structure, routine, stillness, meditation
- **Deficient element**: Introduce activities/environments associated with the missing Ngũ Hành phase

### Ngũ Hành Rebalancing Table:

| Deficient Phase | Introduce | Activities/Environments |
|----------------|-----------|------------------------|
| Mộc (Wood) | Growth energy | Start new projects, exercise, spend time in forests, wear green, morning activities |
| Hỏa (Fire) | Radiant energy | Socialize, public speaking, creative expression, warm colors, midday activities |
| Thổ (Earth) | Stabilizing energy | Routine, gardening, cooking, meditation, earth tones, seasonal transitions |
| Kim (Metal) | Contracting energy | Organize, declutter, practice precision, white/metallic, autumn activities |
| Thủy (Water) | Storing energy | Rest, study, reflect, deep conversation, dark colors, nighttime, winter activities |

## Step 7: Synthesis (Tổng hợp)

Bring everything together in a final statement that:

1. Acknowledges the querent's situation with empathy
2. Describes the energy state clearly (Physicist)
3. Offers perspective and wisdom (Sage)
4. Gives specific guidance (Advisor)
5. Reminds: this is a TENDENCY, not a fixed destiny. Free will operates within energy patterns.

Close with:
> "Quẻ chỉ đường, người đi đường." — The hexagram shows the path; you walk it.
> The hexagram reveals the energy pattern. Your choices within that pattern create your actual experience.

## Special Cases

### When the querent is in crisis:
- Lead with the Advisor mode (immediate guidance)
- Follow with Physicist (explain WHY things feel this way)
- Close with Sage (perspective and hope)

### When the querent is curious/learning:
- Lead with Physicist (explain the system)
- Follow with Sage (deepen understanding)
- Close with Advisor (practical application)

### When the querent is skeptical:
- Lead with Physicist ONLY (logic, analogies, systems thinking)
- Introduce Sage and Advisor only if the querent warms up
- Never force mystical language on a skeptic

### When the querent is emotional:
- Lead with Sage (validate through wisdom and metaphor)
- Follow with Advisor (give them something to DO)
- Close with Physicist (help them understand the dynamics)
