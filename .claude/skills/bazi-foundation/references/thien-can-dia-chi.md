# Thiên Can & Địa Chi Interactions — Tương tác Can Chi

## Thiên Can Combinations (Thiên Can Ngũ Hợp)

When two specific Thiên Can meet, they combine to produce a NEW element:

| Can 1 | Can 2 | Combine To | Condition |
|-------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Giáp (甲) | Kỷ (己) | Thổ | Central harmony — Wood meets soft Earth |
| Ất (乙) | Canh (庚) | Kim | Metal refines gentle Wood |
| Bính (丙) | Tân (辛) | Thủy | Sun melts jewelry into flowing water |
| Đinh (丁) | Nhâm (壬) | Mộc | Candle warms ocean into growth |
| Mậu (戊) | Quý (癸) | Hỏa | Mountain meets rain, creates fire energy |

**Important**: Combination (hợp) doesn't always fully transform. It depends on surrounding elements and season. A combination can:
- Fully transform (hóa) — both stems lose original identity
- Partially combine — attraction but incomplete change
- Be blocked — a third stem interferes

## Địa Chi Clashes (Địa Chi Lục Xung)

Six pairs of branches oppose each other — energy conflict:

| Chi 1 | Chi 2 | Conflict Type | Meaning |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Tý (子) | Ngọ (午) | Thủy vs Hỏa | Water-Fire clash — fundamental tension, emotional turmoil |
| Sửu (丑) | Mùi (未) | Thổ vs Thổ | Earth-Earth — stubbornness, both sides immovable |
| Dần (寅) | Thân (申) | Mộc vs Kim | Wood-Metal clash — growth vs control, career upheaval |
| Mão (卯) | Dậu (酉) | Mộc vs Kim | Wood-Metal — similar to Dần-Thân but more personal |
| Thìn (辰) | Tuất (戌) | Thổ vs Thổ | Earth-Earth — institutional clash, breaking old structures |
| Tị (巳) | Hợi (亥) | Hỏa vs Thủy | Fire-Water — passion vs depth, internal conflict |

**Clash effects**: Disruption, change, relocation, relationship breaks. BUT clashes also UNLOCK frozen energy. Sometimes a clash is exactly what a stagnant chart needs.

## Địa Chi Penalties (Địa Chi Hình)

Penalties are INTERNAL friction — more subtle and persistent than clashes:

### Three Penalties of Ingratitude (Vô Ân Chi Hình)
- **Dần (寅) → Tị (巳) → Thân (申)**: Fire-Metal-Water triangle. Betrayal dynamics, being punished for good deeds.

### Three Penalties of Rudeness (Vô Lễ Chi Hình)
- **Mùi (未) → Sửu (丑) → Tuất (戌)**: Earth triangle. Stubbornness, health issues from worry, social friction.

### Self-Penalty (Tự Hình)
- **Thìn-Thìn (辰辰)**, **Ngọ-Ngọ (午午)**, **Dậu-Dậu (酉酉)**, **Hợi-Hợi (亥亥)**: Same branch doubled = self-destructive tendencies, internal conflict.

## Địa Chi Three Harmonies (Tam Hợp)

Three branches form an elemental alliance — powerful energy when complete:

| Branches | Element Formed | Energy |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Thân-Tý-Thìn (申子辰) | Thủy | Water frame — intellectual, flowing, adaptive |
| Hợi-Mão-Mùi (亥卯未) | Mộc | Wood frame — growth, creativity, expansion |
| Dần-Ngọ-Tuất (寅午戌) | Hỏa | Fire frame — passion, leadership, transformation |
| Tị-Dậu-Sửu (巳酉丑) | Kim | Metal frame — discipline, precision, structure |

**Partial harmonies** (only 2 of 3 present): Still significant — creates affinity toward that element.

---

## Địa Chi Seasonal Harmonies (Tam Hội / Phương Hợp)

Three consecutive seasonal branches amplify one element to maximum:

| Branches | Season | Element | Effect |
|----------|--------|---------|--------|
| Dần-Mão-Thìn (寅卯辰) | Spring | Mộc (peak) | Explosive growth energy |
| Tị-Ngọ-Mùi (巳午未) | Summer | Hỏa (peak) | Maximum radiance and heat |
| Thân-Dậu-Tuất (申酉戌) | Autumn | Kim (peak) | Maximum structure and refinement |
| Hợi-Tý-Sửu (亥子丑) | Winter | Thủy (peak) | Maximum depth and storage |

**Warning**: Seasonal harmonies are the STRONGEST form of elemental concentration. If the chart already has excess of that element, this amplifies the imbalance dramatically.

| Chi 1 | Chi 2 | Type | Meaning |
|-------|-------|------|---------|

| Sửu (丑) | Ngọ (午) | Thổ-Hỏa | Burnout from loyalty, self-sacrifice |
| Dần (寅) | Tị (巳) | Mộc-Hỏa | Ambition backfires, false allies |
| Mão (卯) | Thìn (辰) | Mộc-Thổ | Gentle person exploited, boundaries violated |
| Thân (申) | Hợi (亥) | Kim-Thủy | Over-planning kills action, analysis paralysis |
| Dậu (酉) | Tuất (戌) | Kim-Thổ | Perfectionism meets stubbornness, stalemate |
